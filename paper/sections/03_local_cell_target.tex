\section{The local-cell target}

The local-cell target is the four-state object \(L\).  It must recover the Project 22 generated rows:

\[
\begin{array}{c|c|c}
\mathrm{state} & b & r\\
\hline
O0 & 0 & 0\\
O1 & 0 & 1\\
B0 & 1 & 0\\
B1 & 1 & 1
\end{array}
\]

A successful native provenance derivation should explain all of the following from upstream structure:

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\arabic*)]
\item why there are four local states;
\item why they split into ordinary and branch shells;
\item why each shell has two ranks;
\item why the C readout order is \(O0,O1,B0,B1\);
\item why the anchor readout order is \(B0,O0,O1,B1\);
\item why the C paths and anchor residue sets match Project 22;
\item why the overlap markers match the local-cell theorem;
\item why the anchor node paths close.
\end{enumerate}
