\section{Introduction}

The Gap A work has now split into a clean ladder.

Project 21 isolated the answer-pair generator problem and proved a reduced selector theorem inside a 16-candidate universe.  Project 22 generated the local Lift \& Twist answer-cell kernel.  Project 23 derived the reduced 16-candidate universe from that kernel.

The remaining question is upstream.

Why does the local Lift \& Twist answer cell exist?

This paper names that question as a native provenance problem.  We are not trying to select from a copied reduced universe.  We are not trying to polish the visible diagonal.  We are asking whether native G60 data force the four local states
\[
    O0,\quad O1,\quad B0,\quad B1,
\]
with their C paths, anchor residue sets, overlap markers, and closed anchor node paths.
